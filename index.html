Welcome to Jiangmen

Jiangmen, located in the western coastal region of Guangdong Province, China, is a treasure trove of history, culture, and natural beauty. Known for its rich heritage and vibrant urban life, Jiangmen offers a unique blend of traditional and modern elements.

As the hometown of overseas Chinese, Jiangmen has a special connection with the world, reflected in its unique architecture, culture, and cuisine. The city is famous for the Kaiping Diaolou and Villages, a UNESCO World Heritage site that showcases the unique fusion of Chinese and Western architectural styles.

Whether you're interested in exploring ancient villages, enjoying delicious Cantonese cuisine, or experiencing the warmth of local hospitality, Jiangmen has something to offer every traveler.

Top Attractions

Bird Paradise

A natural reserve famous for its mangrove forest and thousands of birds. Made famous by the Chinese writer Ba Jin's essay "Bird Paradise."

Learn More

Guifeng Mountain National Forest Park

A well-preserved ancient water town that displays the long history and cultural heritage of Jiangmen.

Learn More

Kaiping Peacock Lake National Wetland Park

This wetland park is famous for its clear lake water, lush wetland vegetation, and diverse wildlife. It is an ideal place for bird-watching, photography, and eco-tourism, allowing tourists to get close to nature and enjoy the peace of the wetland.

Naqin Peninsula Geopark & Ocean Park

Naqin Peninsula Geopark & Ocean Park is a highlight of Jiangmen’s coastal scenery. It is famous for its unique coastal landforms such as strange rocks and sea erosion cliffs, and clear blue sea water. Tourists can walk along the coastal plank road, enjoy the magnificent sea view, or take part in marine activities such as snorkeling and beach camping.

Chuanshan Islands

Including Shangchuan Island and Xiachuan Island, Chuanshan Islands are among Jiangmen’s most popular coastal tourist destinations. With long stretches of golden beaches, crystal-clear sea water, and rich marine resources, the islands are perfect for beach holidays, seafood tasting, and water sports like jet skiing and parasailing.

Kaiping Diaolou

These unique watchtowers and villages reflect the architectural style of the Ming and Qing dynasties. A UNESCO World Heritage site that showcases the fusion of Chinese and Western architectural styles.

Local Cuisine

Gujing Roast Goose

Not only is it a benchmark for Guangdong-style roast goose, but it also was officially listed in Guangdong’s Intangible Cultural Heritage Directory in 2012.

Learn More

Taishan Eel Rice

Taishan Eel Rice is the signature delicacy of Taishan, Jiangmen.

Learn More

Enping Laishen Noodles

Enping Laishen Noodles take late-harvest indica rice as their key ingredient. The finished noodles boast a smooth, glossy surface and a delightfully chewy, slippery texture

Learn More

Kaiping Chikan Tofu Dumplings

Kaiping Chikan Tofu Dumplings, a century-old intangible cultural heritage snack in Jiangmen, are made with local fresh tofu wrapped in mud carp paste and fried. Crispy outside, tender inside, they’re savory with a slight spice—bite in for a crunchy crust and juicy tofu, a must-try when visiting Chikan Ancient Town.

Liyue Cured Meat

Liyue Cured Meat is a century-old flavor from Jiangmen, and was listed in Jiangmen’s intangible cultural heritage in 2020. It’s richly savory, oily but not greasy. Its classic clay pot rice—rice soaked with cured meat oil, fragrant and warm—is a must-try in autumn and winter, offering authentic deliciousness.

Qiaodu Coffee

Qiaodu Coffee is Jiangmen’s innovative drink blending overseas Chinese culture—Tangerine Peel Latte is fragrant and mellow, Bitter Gourd Americano is slightly bitter with a sweet aftertaste. Coffee aroma meets local ingredients, offering a unique Jiangmen flavor, perfect for young tourists to check in.

Culture & Heritage

A Rich Tapestry of Traditions

Jiangmen is known as the "Hometown of Overseas Chinese" due to its large number of emigrants who have settled abroad. This has created a unique cultural blend that combines traditional Chinese elements with Western influences.

The city is famous for its distinctive architectural style, particularly the Kaiping Diaolou, which are multi-story defensive village houses that incorporate Chinese and Western structural and decorative forms.

iangmen is also known for its traditional Cantonese opera, lion dances, and various folk arts that are preserved and performed during festivals and celebrations.

Cultural Heritage

Xinhui Cai Li Fo Kung Fu

A famous traditional martial art in southern China, with a history of over 170 years. It combines the strengths of three martial arts styles and is known for its practicality and flexibility

Pancun Lantern Festival

A traditional folk activity in Pancun, Xinhui, held on the 13th day of the first lunar month every year. With colorful lanterns, dragon and lion dances, it attracts many tourists to experience the lively folk atmosphere.

Liyue Dragon Boat

A long-standing dragon boat culture in Liyue Street, Jianghai District. Every Dragon Boat Festival, exciting dragon boat races are held on the river, showcasing the vigorous spirit of the local people.

Xinhui Palm Weaving

A traditional craft with a history of over 1,600 years. Using local palm leaves, craftsmen weave various delicate products such as fans, baskets, and hats, which are both practical and ornamental.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Jiangmen is during autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant and rainfall is minimal. Spring (March to May) is also a good time with mild temperatures.

Transportation

Jiangmen is well-connected by high-speed rail, buses, and ferries. The city has an efficient public transportation system including buses and taxis for getting around.

Accommodation

Jiangmen offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. It's recommended to book in advance during peak travel seasons.

Local Customs

When visiting temples or traditional villages, dress modestly. It's polite to accept tea with both hands when offered. Tipping is not customary in China.

Language

The local language is Cantonese, but Mandarin is widely understood. Learning a few basic phrases in either language can be helpful. English is not commonly spoken outside tourist areas.

Currency

The official currency is the Chinese Yuan (CNY). Credit cards are accepted in major hotels and restaurants, but it's advisable to carry cash for smaller establishments and markets.
